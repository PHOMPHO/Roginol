Introduction

Roginol is a lonely warrior who once served Lord Darigan's military in the golden days of his kingdom, when the city still floated
peacefully and way before the curse of the orb took over their bodies and their minds. As a child, the girl grew up in the peaceful
farms around the Lord's land with her single father, who knew her heart was supposed to be elsewhere, but was afraid of where
that fiery passion inside of her would end up. Always fascinated by the way of the sword and the knights of Darigan, the child,
who did everything she could to become one, ended up as one of the first female generals of Lord Darigan, beginning her job
by protecting the city from the creatures around it. Now, after the red bath left behind by the war, she walks Neopia in search
of answers and a way to redeem herself for the harm she has done to so many.

Relatives

Roginol never had a big happy family, at the farm, the only relative living with her was her father, a hardworking man who wanted only the best for his daughter, teaching everything he learned in his journey about the lands outside of Darigan to the girl in hopes of her never wanting to leave, she was always incredibly interested in those stories, asking questions and dreaming of what the world outside was like, but later he regretted that decision once she showed interest in joining the military and fighting. Their relationship was quite lovable and nurturing to each other, fights were extremely rare. She loved him very much, even if her actions and words had been dismissive for most of their life, which she regrets very much.

Her mother, however, had left not long after the girls birth, and Roginol knows almost nothing about her, and little was known other than what her father could remember. "A beautiful woman with long red hair like the autumn leaves, a smile as bright as the sun in Altador, and a fierce determination to do anything she put her mind into", that was how the man described the older woman, sadly, her whereabouts are unknown. And the girl never really had any drive to seek out information about her mother, always too focused on her training or work, the idea that the woman had "abandoned her" never left her mind either.

Companions

As a child, the only close friend Roginol had was Lesser, a young, almost heroic, man who wanted to be a real life hero like he would hear in the olden stories, their relationship was warm and friendly, even if the other boys would pester the girl for her ways.

Beyond the curse and subsequent to the girl roaming the lands for quite a few years, Roginol met with a young girl that seemed to be living in one of the Citadels old villages on her own, the dirty clothes suggested she was a farm maiden, but her cheerful nature almost made it seem like a curse had never affected the young one at all, even if the marks where still there - darkened hands and horns. Tired of being pestered for many many miles out of their initial meeting location, Roginol decided to let the girl accompany her, even if her instincts at first were to send the girl away in the incessant fear of not being able to protect the young one.

With this unexpected encounter, Roginol continued her adventures, but now with a partner, Wendy Duval, a 14 year old girl full of life and cheerfulness that loved to frolic around alongside the older woman. On their very first stop, while the woman told her to stay hidden under a cape, was a small village close to Brightvalle, the young girl met for the first time those who did not like the spawns of Darigan, an old man confronted Roginol over why she dared to enter their lands and corrupt their surroundings, the calm woman simply bowed and gave her apologies, turning around and trying to leave the town without causing problems when the angry man began to scream and pulled the cape only to reveal the young girl.

Everything after that felt like a scene in slow-motion, the man grabbed the girls arm screaming to the others as she flayed around trying to run away, but with a single swift move, Roginol took Wendy back into her arms, prepared to see the consequences of her action to the end. But the young one plead for them to leave, Roginol turned her back to the village and ran as far as she could.

Rivalries

Even if she meant no harm to others, Roginol did collect quite a few enemies, be it the many villages who wanted absolutely nothing to do with the spawns of Darigan, as they began to be called after the curse had passed, or the roguish people she entered the way of to help someone else. But the biggest one of them all was Talise Caparelli, a wanted criminal who only knew how to hurt others, a dragonic creature that wanted nothing but riches, everything she standed for was the complete opposite of what Roginol believed in, a recipe for disaster had already been written when the two met for the first time.

Roginol had accepted a bounty to find and end a draik who had been messing around and stealing from the village for a very long time, knowing she would find such creature in the surrounding forest she made her way to the dark and damp cavern only to find Talise relishing and laughing in her throne of bones. The lengthy battle that ensued between the two women was like an epic battle of old, but as sleazy as Talise was, she fled the duel only to spite the other one. Since those days the two have been at war whenever they meet.


The World

Neopia is a planet in the Goldilocks Zone of an unknown star system, at an orbital distance to produce years of 365.25 days
(equivalent to Earth). It has a day/night cycle that is 24 hours in length. It supports a wide array of life forms, many species
of which collectively are grouped together as Neopets.'' -- The Book of Ages



Home to many different kinds of creatures, including Neopets and Faeries, Neopia is quite abundant with resources. This world is divided into two main continents. This once calm and ordinary world has seen it's fair share of wars and red baths. The land where Roginol specifically was born in is located in the northeastern part of the southern continent. There, floating in the sky, you may find the Darigan Citadel, ruled by Lord Darigan and it's military force. Once a normal kingdom which was powered by a magical orb of almost infinite power, Darigan Citadel is now cursed. The orb that brought rich and fertile lands was stolen by King Skarl, who sent a conroi in secret to steal said magical power, due to his envy of Darigan Citadel's riches. Unknown to both parties, disrupting the power of the magical object released a terrible curse on the citizens of the Citadel, twisting and turning their bodies and personalities into a dark mess, many of this poor souls went completely berserk, doing away everything in their way, Roginol was one of them.

Ruling Power

His land was plagued by famine and poverty after Meridell stole the magical orb. Bent on revenge Lord Darigan spent many long years tracking down the one thing that could restore peace to his people. When he finally found the orb he began a bitter war that left Meridell devastated as his minions stopped at nothing until they returned the orb to their master. Sadly the orb worked its foul magic on Lord Darigan and corrupted his once honest heart." -- The Gallery of Evil



Ruled by Lord Darigan, the first and still current ruler, who founded the kingdom, he does not have any children, and as was shown after his defeat, a high ranking member of the army takes his place should he die.

The citadel has two other chains of power nominated by him to keep the peace and order inside the boarders, the first of which is a military chain that boasts a large quantity of troupes, wielding magical weapons and warlocks, commanded by General Kass and General Galgarroth, and the second of which is a council, constituted of a Dungeon Master, a Sheriff, a Constable, a Mayor, a Captain, and two Chairmen, who have the power to vote who may keep power in case of the unfortunate demises of the current ruler.

Driven insane by invisible demons, this former general in Lord Darigan's army rose to power after his master's downfall. Through underhand means he slowly took control of the Darigan Citadel that loomed over the happy realm of Meridell, and used his influence to build an army. An army that he would use to try and conquer the lands below." -- The Gallery of Evil

In his brief attempt to rule the Citadel, General Kass tried to buy the loyalty of the council to move the kingdom into another war against Meridell, with this failure, he than began his secondary plan to eliminate anyone who defied him.

Also known as General Galgarrath, he is and was Lord Darigan's right hand man, with a brief trip to the dungeons during Kass's reign. Some may think him evil, but he is only doing what is best for his people.

Culture

In the beginning all citizens of the citadel were relatively normal neopets of all species, happy to be helping a prosperous kingdom grow with their own hands in a very medieval fashion, with farms and local craftsman teaching the younger generations how to keep producing to survive, cultivating the soil and frolicking around it's peaceful meadows. Only a few of the citizens yearned for something more exciting, with most of them joining it's military.

During the curse, however, these citizens were corrupted by the magical powers of the orb, turning them into twisted monsters with a want for red, maniacal and berserk, these once calm and happy people now roamed the lands destroying and pillaging everything in sight.
After the curse subsided most citizens went back to their normal selves, a few of these now try to repair the damage they've done while others turned into scholars to docment the change and history of the land, but many others developed an evil personality that now dominates them, many of these still live within the citadel, giving it a bad reputation among the other kingdoms of Neopia.

Much like the other lands surrounding it, poverty is the norm inside the Citadel after the curse, with the new lack of farmland in the floating fortress life for most citizens is very complicated, with only very simple foods being offered to the population, with the very small cursed garden only being able to offer a few berries. The imports from the land bellow also suffer from the Citadels bad reputation, so the prices are quite high, making the citizens pay a lot in taxes.

Beliefs

Way before the war, as the Citadel grew, most citizens believed the orb of power to be a gift from outer powers that reigned over their land. Worshiping it's power and gifts to the land every year with a grand party were everyone would bring a gift crafted by them, be it a trinket, weapon, or even food. In the aftermath of battle, however, many began to see the orb as a treacherous item, that fooled them into believing it's lies of a prosperous land, now only believing in Lord Darigan and his powers.

Roginol, however, didn't stop believing that an outer power still looks after the land and all of the citizens of the Citadel, but her belief did diminish after everything she had to endure out in the world.

Catalyst Events

After meeting Lesser, the child of a neighboring farmer, who wanted to move out to the Citadel and become as well known as Kass was in the military, the idea started to spin around Roginols head, could she also fight for her kingdom? But no one had never heard of a woman in the military, and wasn't she very weak? The boys teased her for having such a foolish dream, but that only fueled the young girl to prove them wrong.

At the age of 19, the girl enrolled for the first time in the trials for the force, taking with her only a dull sword - given to her by her father on her 15th birthday -, she knocked every opponent down with swiftness and skil, making the heavy steel sword look almost weightless.

Rising in the ranks quite quickly thanks to her skil, Roginol earned the name of 'crimson bird' amongst her peers and comrades because of her flowing red hair and unique way to use a sword, almost as if she was flying in the battlefield.

As the battle against Meridell approached, her ranking was raised to General, but suddenly her head began to spin and turn, hurting like never before. The curse had begun, her body fell to the ground and she contorted her limbs trying to fight against it. However, none of the citizens were able to break free of the curse, a red bath ensued and after the final battle, she regained control of her mind again. What she saw was a horrible sight of war and destruction, from that day on she swore to never again raise her sword in actual battle, but to only use it to help other.

However, that wouldn't be such a simple thing, after the dismal failure of the Citadel to keep itself together, many from the outside began to steer away from the ones affected by the curse, calling them 'the spawns of Darigan', denying to even let them into villages, making Roginol roam around the lands of Neopia doing odd jobs as a mercenary.

Prologue

On the twenty second day of the month of relaxing, a new little creature was hatched in the land surrounding what would soon be known as the Darigan Citadel, this small creature was called Roginol. This small child had always been curious about the outside world and what was beyond the fields that seemed to have no end, the Gardiner Farm was quite spacious and had many different kinds of fruits and vegetables growing under the care of her father, a man who traveled around Neopia in search of farming knowledge.

During his long journey, he had the luck to find his significant other, Roginols mother, a striking woman that had no fear to do as she pleased, her beauty was unmatched, the long, flowing, red hair shaped her sleek face and thunderous blue eyes that seemed to glow with determination, her smile was contagious to everyone around, but her impulsiveness led the woman a tortuous path in life, but it also led to the birth of Roginol, which she was very happy about, but the woman could not commit to spending her life in a farm and doing nothing more than that.

While settling down on the farm to raise the young child, the man felt a happiness he had never experienced before, but little did he know his partner didn't feel as happy, being around the small growing creature who was so eager to listen and learn about everything was able to warm the hearts of both, which delayed the older woman's departure by a whole year. But at that moment, as happy as she felt, she couldn't ignore it anymore, her heart yearned for adventure and adrenaline, hearing that the man felt heartbroken, but still knew he couldn't trap a free bird in a cage and expect them to sing happily forever, so they parted their ways while the small child remained in the farm to learn and grow.

Around the age of 5, Roginol began to meet the other children who lived in the surrounding farming area, one of them being Lesser, a brave young boy who's dream was to become a hero of the now established Citadel of Darigan, always reciting the stories of old and acting them out with the other children. While alone, the two would always sit by the lake, which perched not far into the forest that enclosed the continent and the areas of the land itself, spending long hours and hours just imagining and talking about what it would be like to travel and see so many different places and people.

Behind the scenes the young girl, however, began to develop feelings for the young boy whom she was so close to, but also for the idea of fighting for her people and protecting the lands, so, at the age of 10, she began to train with the boy so both would enter the trails of the lord together, but that did not sit well with the other boys that also wanted to become knights of the military, even if Lesser would fight and argue against them about how backwards they were being, the others would still pester the girl about how she could never enter the military nor fight for anyone. Despite those awful experiences with others, however, her desire to prove those horrible children wrong were only fueled even more, and so began to burn the flame that moved Roginol to fight and train every single day.

During those years, meanwhile, her father was very much against those dreams just as much as the boys, but he could never have stopped her from trying and training to fulfill them, and after noticing that she was not going to stop even if he or the rest of the people didn't approve of it, he finally decided to give into her determination, which reminded him very much of his past significant other, which scared the man to death since the girl was very much growing to be just like her mother. But even shaken by his own fears, at her 15th birthday, he made a very difficult decision to actually outwardly support her during this arduous journey, giving her a brand new steel sword he commissioned off of a friend on the Citadel. More than happy, the girl thanked her father with all her heart, and decided to train even harder to make the old man proud.

Whilst, her feelings for the boy, Lesser, only grew deeper, wraping around her hearth like the thorns of a prickly rose, yet the two also only grew apart with the passage of time, the boys interest hadn't changed at all, but he also knew he wouldn't be able to win standing by the side of someone no one wanted to see succeed, or so was what all of the others started to plant in his head, and while that hurt his feelings just as much, he saw no other way around it, he had to separate himself from Roginol. With a heavy heart that weight like a block of pure silver, the boy confronted her, saying horrible things only to stop her from looking for him ever again, the girl cried with every word that felt like sharp blades breaking her heart into tiny pieces.

Miserable after that encounter and with those heavy feelings in her chest, the girl decided to prove every single one of them all wrong, they surely were very wrong about all of this. And with a broken heart, she continued her training with even more rage and a flame that burned even brighter inside of her, she had no more reason to hold back at all. And as the years passed, that passion the once little girl had showed had only grown deeper and deeper to feel the hole left behind by the young man, and began twisting into a desire to actually prove to the whole world she could be just as good, if not even better than a man with a sword, which horrified and scared the old man very much, he had already lost his significant other for that same impulsiveness, was he going to lose his child as well?

Chapter 1 - The Path to the Citadel

After such a miserable and despair inducing nightmare of a day, Roginol layed her head on the fluffy pillow that adorned her small but comfortable bed, thoughts swirling around her head, how did it get to this? Did the others hate her that much? The girl couldn't answer, and she didn't want an answer, so she blocked those feelings out, she would never speak or think of them ever again. So the mornings came and went, and 4 whole years had already gone by in a flash, the girl had grown in every sense, the muscles in her body ached in pain after such arduous training every single day and night, her worried father couldn't do a thing about it other than pray she would not destroy her body. But the day to end her wait finally came, at 19 years old she was ready to finally face the tests known as the "Trials of Darigan", a series of physical assessments that had to be completed to enter the Citadels military.

Every year a few of the citizens from all over the region competed against one another in the trials, but even fewer were ever able to complete all of them, ending with years were only one or two participants were chosen, but she wasn't afraid anymore, the girl had honed her abilities enough to crush anything on her way, the only thing that could ever stop her was her own father. The man feared for the girls life very much, even if he knew she would never stop, "please my dear, don't do this to yourself...", his words felt like blades of steel, but Roginol couldn't turn back anymore, "I'm sorry father, but I can't let them doubt me any longer", and with those last words, she took the first step out of the Gardiner farm, for the first time she was heading for the Citadel with no intent on coming back.

Her path, however, would not be an easy one, the farms that cultivated the food for the markets on the Citadel where located quite a few miles away from the town itself, but with steady steps the girl made her way through the forest and meadows while her eyes danced around, she knew something was lurking in the shadows of the trees, and her guard was never down. The gigantic creature, a grey werelupe, jumped in a single movement over the bushes with it's claws ready to dig into her, but with a single strike the girl pulled the sword from her back and cut her foe down to the ground. The adrenaline rushed in her veins while her hands shook in desperation, she had never in her life used her skils for anything other than training, with her calm facade shattered by the creature that now blocked the path behind her, the girl feel to her knees, her now stained sword in the ground.

The sword, once light as a feather, now seemed to weight as much as the monster itself, but her conviction quickly snapped back in place, she couldn't dwell on that no more! Her path wasn't getting any shorter, so the girls stride quickened in a hurried state, while her mind processed what had just happened, her body moved on it's on to the Citadels gate. As the forest ended and the path became paved by stone her heart slowed down with her steps, she was almost at the entrance to the city, but something did not feel right. Just as she looked to the side of the road the marks left by heavy boots grabbed her attention in a second, who could have been entering the forest in such an awkward spot? With her curiosity peaked and a bit of time to spare, Roginol entered the clearing left behind by what seemed to be a military unity bearing a coat of arms that seemed quite familiar to her, many thoughts danced around her head, but they were all interrupted by the loud voices of the men approaching the camp.

."I surly wonder what King Skarl even wants with this place", the brunette soldier said laughing, his face dripping wet and his helmet under one of his arms, "It's probably just another meaningless little war, nothing we can't deal with", the other, who seemed to be a lot older than the others, spouted out while putting the helmet back on his head. They hadn't yet noticed her, but the girls body simply couldn't move once again, the news truly were real, Meridell was planning an attack on Darigan, after all. While frozen in time inside her own head, the group of three or four man took notice of the girl, "What do you think you are doing here?", "She has a sword... we shouldn't let out guard down", "it's just a girl, are you really afraid of her?", none of their words, however, reached her ears, but as she turned around without even thinking they all reacted as a treat, they couldn't have imagined she had such strong muscle memory, but whiting seconds, Roginol had already took her sword in her hand and blocked the incoming attack from the brunette soldier, who now dropped his helmet to the ground only to hold his sword with both hands. Even after putting the weight of his whole body on the sword he couldn't move the girls sword a single inch.

."Don't you dare attack me in such an undignified way, you skamelar mutt", the girl uttered through the teeth while pushing her sword forward with so much strength the man was almost kneeling at her feet. Taken aback by her sheer strength the man moved out of the swords way only to almost get hit by it, gaining a new scratch to adorn his shining armour, while letting out a scream of pure dread, "This is no ordinary girl, this but a monster!", turning around once more, Roginol shafted the sword back on it's sheath and resumed her way to the Citadels gate at once. The thoughts in her head were still a jumbled mess of conspiracies, there was no real reason for, what seemed like, a whole order to be scouting around the kingdom other than a war, and there was also no way Lord Darigan hadn't noticed it yet.

As Roginol got closer to the gate, the soldiers stopped her asking for identification, the entrance to the city had always been a long process, but during the trials it seemed to be even more difficult. Showing the two men the letter she had received from the court accepting her entrance on the trials they sneered letting a condescending chuckle out as the girl took the black papered letter back and crossed the gate, "there's no way a woman will ever be able to endure the trials", those words cut the girls heart in tiny pieces, her skils were again being judged by something she had no control over, "I'll prove them all wrong", she muttered to herself while walking and looking at her feet, only to bump into someone else, "I'm so-", her words cut off as she looked up to meet their gaze.

The Inspiration

I can't quite remember or pinpoint when, but I found Roginol in the pound as a blue bruce and adopted her almost immediately. Her name sounds very similar to "rouxinol" (row-shi-nol; or a nightingale in english), and that inspired me to create a specific kind of character, one that had been imprisoned by their own ways of thinking and had to free themselves on their own. Hence her almost endless determination to achieve her goals, which only end up crushing her freedom and life as a whole and making her see herself roaming the world for the first time with no direction, completely free, just like a bird released from a cage. At first, I didn't quite know how to incorporate her into an actual story that would make real sense, but after a bit of brainstorming and looking at a bunch of different pet colors I could maybe paint her as, I landed on a Darigan bruce. The more exaggerated bird-like features made perfect sense for a character that was supposed to be bird-themed.

After choosing her initial color scheme, I began to try a few different designs for her character. The warrior-like design was there from the beginning, but at first I didn't quite know how to incorporate the curse aspect of the darigan lore and hadn't even really started sketching the first few drafts of her story either. Her first ever design was finished on May 19, 2018 on recolor.me, the second one was loosely based on the first design and was finished around a year after, somewhere around july or august of 2019, and a third and last rendition, which is the definitive one, was finished on August 15, 2020.


Just like with my other characters, I feel like there's at least a small piece of myself that ends up on them without me even realizing it at all and, with Roginol it wasn't any different. For many years during my childhood and teenage years I saw myself locked up in my own head because of mental and health related issues I didn't understand and didn't even know I had to begin with, and on top of that, after high school, being forced to take a big step on my own without any guidance was so incredibly hard. Going to college? I didn't know if I wanted to. Or what I wanted to study specifically. Getting a job? I still had no idea what I was good at. With those predicaments swirling inside my head. I ended up in many toxic environments, with even worse people, before finding the path I wanted to take. However, that took many years and a lot of energy out of me, so much so it was one of the many reasons I left the site for as long as I did, since I had to go through a mental health and self discovery journey was incredibly exhausting and draining. Which ends up making her story have quite a bit of my own experiences and feelings slightly twisted into a more medieval and fantastical setting.

But not everything is melancholy. I did have quite a bit of other sources of inspiration other than my own fluffed head. When it came to the more fantastical aspects of her story, the biggest one for sure stands to be the mangá Claymore, one of my favorites when it comes to medieval fantasy, that may have been a bit obvious to anyone who knows a bit of the story after I mentioned how her encounter with Wendy went. The "cursed" aspect of the Citadels lore really made me remember how the warriors in that story where put aside by society as a whole even if they were so important for their survival. Roginols determination's also does come a bit from the main character, which is shown to actually be a lot weaker than her peers.

On the other side of that spectrum, my honorable mentions for actual setting would be: Berserk, The Elder Scrolls, Drakengard and Diablo games, and The Lord of the Rings books and movies. Most of these (like Elder Scrolls and Lord of the Rings) may sound like very cliche inspirations for a medieval themed story, but with the Lord of the Rings books being one of my favorites, so much so that I own a copy of the compilation of all of the books, which I've read quite a few times over as well, it wouldn't make sense not to include it. While the other three (Berserk, Drakengard and Diablo) helped me quite a lot with the feeling of a fantastical and somber setting this story would take after the battles and the curse as a whole, since I doubt the world would stay the same happy little place it was after such devastation and war. Surely Roginol wouldn't have been the only one affected by their doings during the curse. I also can't imagine Meridell and Brightvale would be comfortable with the idea of having anyone from Darigan around their kingdoms either.

Also, thanks to RE:Creation 2020 I was able to flesh out an actual page for her! Putting all these ideas onto the world has been quite a ride, having to actually workout all of the lose ideas I had for so long motivated me to achieve a lot more with my own characters and pages as a whole. So as a closing note on my inspirations and story behind Roginols character I would like to thank everyone who participated on RE:C, that gave feedback on this page and motivated me to finish.

About Me

My name is Abyss and my pronouns are they/them, I'm a non-binary mortuary sciences student living in Brazil. I like writing, playing video games, cooking, collecting avatars, creating characters, photography and fashion. My favorite genre of media is horror, I love watching horror movies and playing horror themed games, and I would say my favorites in order are Army of Darkness and Silent Hill 2. In real life I have three cutesy little cats, Samara (10yo), Dothi (2yo) and Valeska (8mo) and a significant other that has been quite a light of hope in my life.

I've been on neopets since around 2009 and my journey with writing and creating characters began right here on this website! My first few characters were very inspired by (almost carbon copies of) Twilight, which, at the time, was very much my favorite book, but as I aged and was lead to explore other types of media I began to like other things, my writing style and my characters evolved alongside me and their stories were driven to be more dark and mature, which I began exploring off site and this prompted me to stay away from neopets for a few years (around 2012-2015), to not only explore my passion with more violent and real characters but also to hone my writing skils.

During this period I stayed away from the site, many things changed in my life, not only mental health related topics but also other major life changes, I always had this inexplicable feeling of being an alien amidst my peers, and among the other kids in my school at the time I was seen as a weirdo and a creep. This feelings of isolation and anguish were one of the main reasons my characters turned from "weird little goofbals" to "traumatized children fighting against a society that doesn't accommodate for them", the later being a feeling I don't believe I've overcame just yet. With this swirling thoughts inside my head I began to write my most extensive piece during this hiatus, Memories of Creation, a dissertation that helped me deal with a lot of religious trauma, idealization and other horrible life experiences, and included not only a few of the characters I had began to create on neopets but completely new characters that I began to develop because of it.

Alongside the complete change in tone that overcame my writing style I also began to see these characters more like realistic depictions of people instead of this invincible creatures that could only exist in pure fantasy, even if fantasy elements where embedded into the story itself. This change in tone can still be seen in newest characters like Roginol and Tali, for example, they are both obviously toned down to accommodate for the rules on the site, but pieces of it still stand.

After this period away from the website I returned only to find my old account had been terminated, which did sting me a little bit at the time but also made me realize that those characters would live on in my work no matter what, so why not take this new found opportunity to start over and have a fresh beginning? With this mindset I created sterneflugel, the junction of two german words I always though had a nice sound to them, sterne being star and flugel being wing.

Right now, my main goals are to develop my characters more and make dedicated petpages for most of them, collecting avatars and overall improve my account. You can find my portfolio here and my character directory here, you can talk to me about anything you want to here.
