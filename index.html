about

Roginol is a lonely warrior who once served Lord Darigan's military in the golden days of his kingdom, before the city floated omnosly in the sky and way before the curse of the Orb took over their bodies and their minds.

Roginol is a quiet and gloomy woman who generally prefers to not stand out, but because of the curse, her mental state had declined significantly. She has an uncontrollable urge to not hurt others, which causes paranoia. Deep down, she is a more of a hopeless romantic type of person - dreaming of a world of peace and equality. When happy she smiles softly, which many would say does not fit in with her tough demeanor, and while under stress she tends to toughen her stance and poise in an attempt to hide her anxious thoughts. With an emotionless, almost sad, face most of the time, she only emotes in extreme situations.

Even after so many years after the end of the war, she still sees no way of reparing the horrible reputation gained after the curse. She can still vividly remember parts of the tragedy and the lives she took while being influenced by curse. It very much still haunts her nightmares and has lead to the feeling of guilt never trully vanishing, no matter how much good she may give to the world.

Seeing her life as nothing more than spendable, in the last few years she has decided to dedicate the herself to protecting a young girl she found while traveling around the country-side. Feeling guilty for the lonely life of the girl, they both seet out in a journey together as roginol saw the exchange as a way to use her power and skill in a less destructive way.

background

As a child, The girl grew up in the peaceful farms around the Lord's land with her single father, who knew her heart was supposed to be elsewhere, but was afraid of where that fiery passion inside of her would end up. Always fascinated by the way of the sword and the knights of Darigan, the child, who did everything she could to become one, ended up as one of the first female generals of Lord Darigan, beginning her job by protecting the city from the creatures around it. Now, after the red bath left behind by the war, she walks Neopia in search of answers and a way to redeem herself for the harm she has done to so many.

while in the army she rose quickly through the ranks thanks to her incredible skill, which didn't go unoticed by her peers. while many saw her as nothing more than a show-off and not deserving of her acolades, another much smaller group in the heigher ranks saw the potential of using such skill for their military campains.

the imminent war against meridell proved her ascension to be in good timing as not long after The Orb that once brought prosperity and happiness was stolen. what seemed like nothing soon turned sour as a horrible curse befell all those who carried the darigan blood in their veins. turning military and civilians alike.

those affected showed increased hostility and a hivemind-like behaviour in search of The Orb. nothing could stand in their way and using their hands or any other tools at their disposal the people of darigan attacked their neighboring lands in a frenzy.

as the curse was broken, the changes in their bodies remained. the scars, horns and dark marks of the afflicted stood the test of time, segregating them from the rest of the world.

Prologue

On the twenty second day of the month of relaxing, a new little creature was hatched in the land surrounding what would soon be known as the Darigan Citadel, this small creature was called Roginol. This small child had always been curious about the outside world and what was beyond the fields that seemed to have no end, the Gardiner Farm was quite spacious and had many different kinds of fruits and vegetables growing under the care of her father, a man who traveled around Neopia in search of farming knowledge.

During his long journey, he had the luck to find his significant other, Roginols mother, a striking woman that had no fear to do as she pleased, her beauty was unmatched, the long, flowing, red hair shaped her sleek face and thunderous blue eyes that seemed to glow with determination, her smile was contagious to everyone around, but her impulsiveness led the woman a tortuous path in life, but it also led to the birth of Roginol, which she was very happy about, but the woman could not commit to spending her life in a farm and doing nothing more than that.

While settling down on the farm to raise the young child, the man felt a happiness he had never experienced before, but little did he know his partner didn't feel as happy, being around the small growing creature who was so eager to listen and learn about everything was able to warm the hearts of both, which delayed the older woman's departure by a whole year. But at that moment, as happy as she felt, she couldn't ignore it anymore, her heart yearned for adventure and adrenaline, hearing that the man felt heartbroken, but still knew he couldn't trap a free bird in a cage and expect them to sing happily forever, so they parted their ways while the small child remained in the farm to learn and grow.

Around the age of 5, Roginol began to meet the other children who lived in the surrounding farming area, one of them being Lesser, a brave young boy who's dream was to become a hero of the now established Citadel of Darigan, always reciting the stories of old and acting them out with the other children. While alone, the two would always sit by the lake, which perched not far into the forest that enclosed the continent and the areas of the land itself, spending long hours and hours just imagining and talking about what it would be like to travel and see so many different places and people.

Behind the scenes the young girl, however, began to develop feelings for the young boy whom she was so close to, but also for the idea of fighting for her people and protecting the lands, so, at the age of 10, she began to train with the boy so both would enter the trails of the lord together, but that did not sit well with the other boys that also wanted to become knights of the military, even if Lesser would fight and argue against them about how backwards they were being, the others would still pester the girl about how she could never enter the military nor fight for anyone. Despite those awful experiences with others, however, her desire to prove those horrible children wrong were only fueled even more, and so began to burn the flame that moved Roginol to fight and train every single day.

During those years, meanwhile, her father was very much against those dreams just as much as the boys, but he could never have stopped her from trying and training to fulfill them, and after noticing that she was not going to stop even if he or the rest of the people didn't approve of it, he finally decided to give into her determination, which reminded him very much of his past significant other, which scared the man to death since the girl was very much growing to be just like her mother. But even shaken by his own fears, at her 15th birthday, he made a very difficult decision to actually outwardly support her during this arduous journey, giving her a brand new steel sword he commissioned off of a friend on the Citadel. More than happy, the girl thanked her father with all her heart, and decided to train even harder to make the old man proud.

Whilst, her feelings for the boy, Lesser, only grew deeper, wraping around her hearth like the thorns of a prickly rose, yet the two also only grew apart with the passage of time, the boys interest hadn't changed at all, but he also knew he wouldn't be able to win standing by the side of someone no one wanted to see succeed, or so was what all of the others started to plant in his head, and while that hurt his feelings just as much, he saw no other way around it, he had to separate himself from Roginol. With a heavy heart that weight like a block of pure silver, the boy confronted her, saying horrible things only to stop her from looking for him ever again, the girl cried with every word that felt like sharp blades breaking her heart into tiny pieces.

Miserable after that encounter and with those heavy feelings in her chest, the girl decided to prove every single one of them all wrong, they surely were very wrong about all of this. And with a broken heart, she continued her training with even more rage and a flame that burned even brighter inside of her, she had no more reason to hold back at all. And as the years passed, that passion the once little girl had showed had only grown deeper and deeper to feel the hole left behind by the young man, and began twisting into a desire to actually prove to the whole world she could be just as good, if not even better than a man with a sword, which horrified and scared the old man very much, he had already lost his significant other for that same impulsiveness, was he going to lose his child as well?

Chapter 1 - The Path to the Citadel

After such a miserable and despair inducing nightmare of a day, Roginol layed her head on the fluffy pillow that adorned her small but comfortable bed, thoughts swirling around her head, how did it get to this? Did the others hate her that much? The girl couldn't answer, and she didn't want an answer, so she blocked those feelings out, she would never speak or think of them ever again. So the mornings came and went, and 4 whole years had already gone by in a flash, the girl had grown in every sense, the muscles in her body ached in pain after such arduous training every single day and night, her worried father couldn't do a thing about it other than pray she would not destroy her body. But the day to end her wait finally came, at 19 years old she was ready to finally face the tests known as the "Trials of Darigan", a series of physical assessments that had to be completed to enter the Citadels military.

Every year a few of the citizens from all over the region competed against one another in the trials, but even fewer were ever able to complete all of them, ending with years were only one or two participants were chosen, but she wasn't afraid anymore, the girl had honed her abilities enough to crush anything on her way, the only thing that could ever stop her was her own father. The man feared for the girls life very much, even if he knew she would never stop, "please my dear, don't do this to yourself...", his words felt like blades of steel, but Roginol couldn't turn back anymore, "I'm sorry father, but I can't let them doubt me any longer", and with those last words, she took the first step out of the Gardiner farm, for the first time she was heading for the Citadel with no intent on coming back.

Her path, however, would not be an easy one, the farms that cultivated the food for the markets on the Citadel where located quite a few miles away from the town itself, but with steady steps the girl made her way through the forest and meadows while her eyes danced around, she knew something was lurking in the shadows of the trees, and her guard was never down. The gigantic creature, a grey werelupe, jumped in a single movement over the bushes with it's claws ready to dig into her, but with a single strike the girl pulled the sword from her back and cut her foe down to the ground. The adrenaline rushed in her veins while her hands shook in desperation, she had never in her life used her skils for anything other than training, with her calm facade shattered by the creature that now blocked the path behind her, the girl feel to her knees, her now stained sword in the ground.

The sword, once light as a feather, now seemed to weight as much as the monster itself, but her conviction quickly snapped back in place, she couldn't dwell on that no more! Her path wasn't getting any shorter, so the girls stride quickened in a hurried state, while her mind processed what had just happened, her body moved on it's on to the Citadels gate. As the forest ended and the path became paved by stone her heart slowed down with her steps, she was almost at the entrance to the city, but something did not feel right. Just as she looked to the side of the road the marks left by heavy boots grabbed her attention in a second, who could have been entering the forest in such an awkward spot? With her curiosity peaked and a bit of time to spare, Roginol entered the clearing left behind by what seemed to be a military unity bearing a coat of arms that seemed quite familiar to her, many thoughts danced around her head, but they were all interrupted by the loud voices of the men approaching the camp.

."I surly wonder what King Skarl even wants with this place", the brunette soldier said laughing, his face dripping wet and his helmet under one of his arms, "It's probably just another meaningless little war, nothing we can't deal with", the other, who seemed to be a lot older than the others, spouted out while putting the helmet back on his head. They hadn't yet noticed her, but the girls body simply couldn't move once again, the news truly were real, Meridell was planning an attack on Darigan, after all. While frozen in time inside her own head, the group of three or four man took notice of the girl, "What do you think you are doing here?", "She has a sword... we shouldn't let out guard down", "it's just a girl, are you really afraid of her?", none of their words, however, reached her ears, but as she turned around without even thinking they all reacted as a treat, they couldn't have imagined she had such strong muscle memory, but whiting seconds, Roginol had already took her sword in her hand and blocked the incoming attack from the brunette soldier, who now dropped his helmet to the ground only to hold his sword with both hands. Even after putting the weight of his whole body on the sword he couldn't move the girls sword a single inch.

."Don't you dare attack me in such an undignified way, you skamelar mutt", the girl uttered through the teeth while pushing her sword forward with so much strength the man was almost kneeling at her feet. Taken aback by her sheer strength the man moved out of the swords way only to almost get hit by it, gaining a new scratch to adorn his shining armour, while letting out a scream of pure dread, "This is no ordinary girl, this but a monster!", turning around once more, Roginol shafted the sword back on it's sheath and resumed her way to the Citadels gate at once. The thoughts in her head were still a jumbled mess of conspiracies, there was no real reason for, what seemed like, a whole order to be scouting around the kingdom other than a war, and there was also no way Lord Darigan hadn't noticed it yet.

As Roginol got closer to the gate, the soldiers stopped her asking for identification, the entrance to the city had always been a long process, but during the trials it seemed to be even more difficult. Showing the two men the letter she had received from the court accepting her entrance on the trials they sneered letting a condescending chuckle out as the girl took the black papered letter back and crossed the gate, "there's no way a woman will ever be able to endure the trials", those words cut the girls heart in tiny pieces, her skils were again being judged by something she had no control over, "I'll prove them all wrong", she muttered to herself while walking and looking at her feet, only to bump into someone else, "I'm so-", her words cut off as she looked up to meet their gaze.

Wendy sat down at the edge of the river, submerging her feet on the running water while Roginol took off the scattered plates of armor from her body, "don't you regret going trough so much to achieve your dreams only to end up like this?", the young girl asked, turning her head slightly to see the other, she thought about her years training in the farm, the trials, Lord Darigan, the war, she didn't regret any of it, the misery of it weighed heavy on her heart, but she wouldn't have changed her decision, "no, not at all", the woman answered while sitting on the ground, her back propped against a big and old tree.

Confused the young girl scrunched her face in disapproval, "is there nothing about all of this that you regret? at all?", the denial was quite evident on her voice, which took the woman by surprise, "if I always stopped to anlyse and regret the past I would never walk forward at all", but the succinct answer only confused Wendy even more, "is there nothing you actually regret?", her tone had shifted from absolute denial to complete curiosity, the older one could never understand how that child was so expressive after witnessing such tragedy, but thinking back to her younger days she was also quite expressive, wasn't she?

Maybe there are one or two things I regret not saying", Roginol answered honestly, the two people that came to her head when thinking about regret had always been the only two men in her life who mattered more than the fighting or the work - her father and Lesser. Looking up at the scattered sunshine raining down on her through the leaves she left out a chuckle, "I imagine you are waiting to hear what those were, right?", nodding attentively, Wendy scooted herself over and heard attentively to the tale.

."While I still lived in the farm with my father we had only a single fight, I was about 19 years old at the time, the post boy had just delivered my black letter, the coat of arms that adorned wax seal on the envelope made it clear that I had been accepted for the trials, he had always disprove of my ways, but he now had reason to act on them and convince me not to go. During that day I hid the letter under my pillow, scared of his reaction, but it didn't take long before he found it and came my way to question it. For the first time in my whole life I heard fathers stern voice shout in my direction, 'you know how dangerous this is, how dare you hide it from me', he shouted while pointing to the letter, I screamed back, cursing him for wanting to stop me, no matter what he or any other said to me, I had decided to take that burden upon myself. That night I slept with a heavy heart, and the next day I parted to the Citadel... at the time I was so angry I didn't say a word to him, the only times we talked were thought letters, horrible letters were I could not convey how sorry I was for not telling him how much I loved him for everything he had done.

With her jaw slightly dropped and the depth of regret on her Roginols voice, Wendy nudged herself closer to the older woman and laid her fluffy hair on her shoulder, "I'm sure he knew how much you loved him", the young girl said with a reassuring smile on her face, "I sure hope so... because I never had the courage to say it to his face, and in the end... by my hands... he would never be able to hear it", almost as if a flash of memory overcame her sight, Roginol looked at her hands that seemed to flash back to the old Gardiner state, covered in red liquid and back again to reality in the forest.
